Akhenaten Museum

sat, Nov 9th 2024

The Akhenaten Museum is a museum under construction in the city of Minya. Work on the project began in 2002, and it is the third largest museum in Egypt and the largest museum in Upper Egypt. It documents the history of the pharaohs in Minya, the story of the city of “Akhet Ton” known as Amarna Hill, the story of King Akhenaten with Queen Nefertiti, and the idea of ​​uniting the goddess with this. The city where Akhenaten lived for about 17 years. Construction could not be completed due to financing problems.


Beni Hassan Tombs

sat, Nov 9th 2024

Beni Hassan is an archaeological area containing an ancient Egyptian cemetery. It is located about 20 kilometers south of the city of Minya in the region known as Middle Egypt, extending between Assiut and Memphis. Although there are some tombs from the Old Kingdom era in the cemetery, this cemetery was mainly used during the Middle Kingdom era, which extended from the 21st century BC to the 17th century BC.


Tell el-Amarna

sat, Nov 9th 2024

Tell el-Amarna is the new capital established by King Akhenaten, and it is located forty-five kilometers south of the Beni Hassan Tombs in Minya Governorate. The remains of the ancient capital still exist today.
